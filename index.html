What are Ultra-Processed Foods?

Ultra-processed foods (UPFs) are industrial formulations typically containing five or more ingredients that include additives not commonly found in home cooking, such as preservatives, emulsifiers, sweeteners, and artificial colors.

These foods often contain high amounts of sugar, unhealthy fats, and salt, while being low in fiber, protein, and micronutrients. Research has linked regular consumption of ultra-processed foods to various health issues.

UPF Detector app helps you easily and quickly identify these foods while shopping, empowering you to make healthier choices for you and your family.

Common Examples of UPFs:

  • • Packaged snacks and chips
  • • Instant noodles and soups
  • • Many breakfast cereals
  • • Carbonated soft drinks
  • • Processed meats (hot dogs, nuggets)
  • • Ready-to-heat meals
  • • Mass-produced packaged breads
